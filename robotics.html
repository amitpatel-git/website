What is Robotics?

Robotics is an interdisciplinary field that combines computer science, engineering, and technology to design, construct, and operate robots.

Introduction to Robotics

Robotics involves the design, construction, operation, and use of robots to perform tasks traditionally done by humans. Robots are used in manufacturing, healthcare, space exploration, and more.

Learn More

Key Components

Robots consist of mechanical parts, sensors, actuators, and control systems. AI and machine learning enable robots to make decisions and adapt to environments.

Learn More

Applications

From industrial automation to surgical robots and self-driving cars, robotics is transforming industries and improving quality of life worldwide.

Major Fields in Robotics

Explore the diverse specializations within robotics engineering and research.

Industrial Robotics

Design and implementation of robots for manufacturing, assembly, and production lines.

Medical Robotics

Development of surgical robots, prosthetics, and rehabilitation devices.

Autonomous Vehicles

Self-driving cars, drones, and other autonomous transportation systems.

AI & Machine Learning

Implementing artificial intelligence to enable robot decision-making.

Space Robotics

Robots for space exploration, satellite servicing, and planetary rovers.

Service Robotics

Domestic robots, cleaning robots, and personal assistant robots.

How to Build a Career in Robotics

A step-by-step guide to becoming a robotics engineer or researcher.

1

Build Strong Fundamentals

Master mathematics, physics, and programming (Python, C++). Study mechanical and electrical engineering concepts.

2

Pursue Relevant Education

Consider degrees in Robotics Engineering, Mechatronics, Computer Science, or Electrical/Mechanical Engineering with robotics specialization.

3

Develop Technical Skills

Learn CAD tools (SolidWorks), robotics frameworks (ROS), control systems, and AI/ML algorithms.

4

Work on Practical Projects

Build robots, participate in competitions (FIRST Robotics, RoboCup), and contribute to open-source robotics projects.

5

Gain Industry Experience

Intern at robotics companies, join research labs, or work on industrial automation projects.

6

Specialize & Advance

Choose a niche (computer vision, motion planning, etc.) and consider graduate studies for research roles.
